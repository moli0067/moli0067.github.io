Life of a Sea Turtle

Sea turtles have spent millions of years in Earth's oceans because they exist as marine reptiles. They spend their life underwater but require surface swimming to breathe. Sea turtles need to travel long distances because they search for food and return to their nesting sites.

Sea turtles play an important role in ocean ecosystems. Some species of turtles help maintain healthy seagrass beds while other species protect the balance of marine food chains. Their movement across various ocean regions enables them to improve the ecological health of multiple marine environments.

Today, sea turtles face many threats which include plastic pollution and fishing nets and habitat loss and climate change. The study of sea turtles enables people to comprehend the importance of ocean conservation.

Facts About Sea Turtles

  • Sea turtles breathe air, so they must surface regularly.
  • They can migrate thousands of kilometers between feeding and nesting areas.
  • Many sea turtles return to the same beaches where they were born to lay eggs.
  • Plastic is dangerous because turtles may confuse it with jellyfish.
